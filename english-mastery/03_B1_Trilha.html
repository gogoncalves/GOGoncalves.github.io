Unit 01 / 08 · The killer

Present Perfect — formação & 4 usos.

Em PT a gente fala "Eu trabalho aqui há 3 anos" usando presente. Em EN você obrigatoriamente usa Present Perfect: "I have worked here for 3 years" / "I have been working here". Esse tempo conecta passado ao presente — algo que o português faz com presente puro.

A fórmula

FormaEstruturaExemplo tech
Afirmativahave/has + past participleI have shipped 5 features.
Negativahaven't/hasn't + past participleShe hasn't reviewed it yet.
PerguntaHave/Has + sujeito + past participleHave you deployed?
Resposta curtaYes, I have. / No, I haven't.NÃO "Yes, I do" para PP!

Unit 02 / 08 · The decisive duel

PP vs Past Simple — o duelo decisivo.

Quando usar Past Simple ("I deployed yesterday") vs Present Perfect ("I have deployed")? A regra é única, mas ferina. Erro nº 1 dos brasileiros B1+. Domina aqui e o resto vira fácil.

A regra de ouro

Use Past Simple quando...Use Present Perfect quando...
Tempo DEFINIDO no passado (yesterday, last week, in 2020, ago, when I was at Google)Tempo INDEFINIDO ou conexão com o presente
Período TERMINADO (this morning, depois das 12h)Período NÃO TERMINADO (this morning, antes das 12h)
Sequência de eventos passadosExperiência de vida (ever, never)
"What happened?""What's happened?" (resultado importa agora)

⚠ A regra que separa B1 de B2

Se você diz QUANDO exatamente algo aconteceu → Past Simple. Se NÃO diz, ou tempo é vago/atual → Present Perfect.

Past Simple — tempo definido
I deployed yesterday.
We shipped last sprint.
The bug broke prod in 2023.
Present Perfect — tempo indefinido
I 've deployed 30 times. (sem dizer quando)
We 've shipped a lot. (em geral)
The bug has broken prod twice. (até hoje)

⚠ Confusão clássica: "ago" + "for"

❌ Mistura
I have started 3 years ago.
I started here for 3 years.
✅ Combos certos
I started 3 years ago. (Past Simple + ago)
I have worked here for 3 years. (PP + for)

Unit 03 / 08 · Duration

FOR / SINCE — career duration.

"For 3 years" vs "Since 2020". O FOR + período (3 years, 5 minutes, a long time). SINCE + ponto no tempo (2020, last week, I joined). Ferramenta essencial pra entrevistas, intros e tenure conversations.

Use FOR + ...Use SINCE + ...
duração / período de tempoponto inicial no tempo
for 3 years · for 6 months · for a long time · for agessince 2020 · since Monday · since I joined · since lunch
"How long have you been here?" → "For 3 years.""When did you start?" → "Since 2020."

⚠ Brasileiro confunde FOR com SINCE

❌ Errado
I've worked here since 5 years.
The bug has been there for Monday.
✅ Correto
I've worked here for 5 years.
The bug has been there since Monday.

Unit 04 / 08 · Promises

First Conditional — sprint promises.

"If we deploy on Friday, we'll need on-call". Situação real, possível, no futuro. Em planning meetings você usa o tempo todo. Erro mortal: nunca "if + will".

A fórmula

Cláusula IFCláusula resultadoExemplo tech
If + present simplewill + verboIf we deploy Friday, we'll need on-call.
If + presentcan / may / might + VIf you have time, you might want to refactor.
If + presentimperativoIf you see the alert, page me.
Unless = if notwill + VWe won't deploy unless tests pass.

⚠ NUNCA "if + will"

❌ Erro brasileiro
If we will deploy Friday, we will need on-call.
✅ Correto
If we deploy Friday, we 'll need on-call.
Após IF, sempre present simple (mesmo falando do futuro). O "will" só aparece na outra cláusula.

Unit 05 / 08 · Relaying

Reported Speech — relaying meetings.

"O CTO disse que vamos refatorar". "Ela perguntou se o deploy estava pronto". Em tech você relata decisões o tempo todo: status update, async docs, postmortem. Regra-chave: recue um tempo verbal.

O recuo dos tempos

DirectReported
"I am tired" (Present Simple)She said she was tired.
"I'm working on it" (Present Continuous)He said he was working on it.
"I deployed" (Past Simple)She said she had deployed.
"I have shipped" (Present Perfect)He said he had shipped.
"I will deploy" (Future will)She said she would deploy.
"I can fix it" (modal can)He said he could fix it.
"I must leave" (modal must)She said she had to leave.

Say vs Tell

VerboEstruturaExemplo
saysay (sem objeto direto)She said the deploy is ready.
telltell + pessoa (sempre com)She told me the deploy is ready.
askask + pessoaHe asked me about the timeline.

⚠ Pergunta indireta — sem inversão!

Em pergunta direta: "Where are you?" (with inversion). Em reported question: "He asked where I was" — a ordem volta pro normal sujeito-verbo. SEM "?".

Unit 07 / 08 · ING vs TO

Gerund vs Infinitive — qual depois do verbo?

"I want to deploy" mas "I enjoy deploying". Por quê? Cada verbo "manda" no que vem depois. Decora os padrões.

Pedem ING (gerund)Pedem TO + V (infinitive)Pedem AMBOS
enjoy, finish, avoid, suggest, recommend, miss, practice, keep, can't help, can't stand, look forward towant, would like, plan, decide, hope, expect, promise, refuse, agree, manage, learn, need, offer, try (esforço)like, love, hate, prefer, start, begin, continue

⚠ "look forward to" — o TO é PREPOSIÇÃO, não infinitivo!

Errado: I look forward to see you
Certo: I look forward to seeing you.

Mesmo padrão: be used to + ING, get used to + ING.

Unit 08 / 08 · Passive intro

Passive Voice — describing systems.

"O bug foi reportado pelo usuário". "O deploy foi aprovado". Em postmortems e docs técnicos, passive voice é dominante porque foca no processo/sistema, não em quem fez. Tech docs adoram passive.

Estrutura: be + past participle

TempoPassiveExemplo tech
Present Simpleam/is/are + p.p.The build is triggered automatically.
Past Simplewas/were + p.p.The deploy was approved yesterday.
Present Perfecthave/has been + p.p.The bug has been fixed.
Future willwill be + p.p.The release will be scheduled.
Modalmodal + be + p.p.It must be tested.
