Writing Lab.

Pratique writing real do dia-a-dia tech: standup update, code review comment, postmortem, RFC, performance review. Cada prompt tem cenário, tarefa, rubrica de auto-avaliação e exemplo de boa resposta. Por nível CEFR.

Como usar

Cada prompt: leia o cenário → escreva sua resposta na textarea → confira contra a rubrica → compare com o exemplo. Não copie o exemplo. Escreva primeiro, compare depois. O ganho está no esforço de produzir, não em ler resposta pronta.

P01Nível A1Self-introduction em DM Slack
Cenário: Você acabou de entrar num time novo. Manda DM no Slack pro tech lead se apresentando. 3-4 linhas.
Sua tarefa: escreva o DM. Inclua:
  • Saudação apropriada
  • Seu nome, role, anos de experiência
  • Tech stack que você conhece
  • Convite cordial pra conversar
0 palavras

★ Rubrica de auto-avaliação

  • ✓
    "Hi [name]" ou "Hey [name]" — saudação casual de Slack (não "Dear")
  • ✓
    Identidade com "I'm + role + at" não "I am engineer" (artigo obrigatório!)
  • ✓
    Tempo de experiência com "I've been working with X for Y years" (Present Perfect)
  • ✓
    Convite cordial: "Looking forward to working with you" ou "Happy to chat anytime"
  • ✓
    SEM "Dear", "I am writing to" — formal demais pra Slack

★ Exemplo de boa resposta

Hey Maria,

I'm Gustavo — just joined the squad as a senior backend engineer. I've been working with Go and Postgres for 6 years, mostly in fintech. Excited to collaborate on the auth migration!

Happy to chat anytime — DM me or grab time on my calendar.

Cheers,
Gustavo
P02Nível A2Daily standup update (async)
Cenário: Time async, standup escrito no Slack às 10h. Inclua: o que fez ontem, o que vai fazer hoje, blockers.
Sua tarefa: 3-5 linhas curtas.
  • Ontem: 1-2 conquistas (Past Simple)
  • Hoje: 1-2 planos (going to / will)
  • Blockers: explicite ou diga "no blockers"
0 palavras

★ Rubrica

  • ✓
    Past Simple correto: "shipped", "deployed", "fixed" — não "shipping" pro passado
  • ✓
    "Going to" pra plano definido: "Going to start the migration"
  • ✓
    "Will" pra commitments: "I'll review your PR"
  • ✓
    Blockers explícitos ou "No blockers" — não fingir que tudo tá bem
  • ✓
    Bullet points / formato escaneável (não parágrafos densos)

★ Exemplo

**Yesterday:**
- Shipped the new dashboard to staging
- Fixed the flaky test in CI

**Today:**
- Going to kick off the auth refactor
- Will review @maria's PR

**Blockers:** none
P03Nível B1Code review comment construtivo
Cenário: Você revisa um PR de um colega júnior. O código funciona mas tem um bug sutil de race condition. Comentário: cordial + assertivo + educacional.
Sua tarefa: 3-5 frases.
  • Reconheça algo positivo do PR (1 linha)
  • Aponte o problema específico
  • Sugira a correção (não imponha)
  • Ofereça ajuda ou recurso
0 palavras

★ Rubrica

  • ✓
    Abre com positivo: "Nice work on..." ou "Solid approach overall"
  • ✓
    Aponta problema com hedging: "I think we might have" ou "There might be" — não "This is wrong"
  • ✓
    Sugere alternativa: "Have you considered..." ou "Would it work to..."
  • ✓
    Oferece ajuda: "Happy to pair on this" ou "Let me know if you want to chat"
  • ✓
    NÃO usa "obviously", "wrong", "you should know" — soa hostil

★ Exemplo

Nice work on this! The structure is clean.

One thing I noticed: I think we might have a race condition here when two requests hit the cache simultaneously. The current logic reads then writes without a lock.

Have you considered using a mutex or a CAS operation? Something like sync.Map could simplify this.

Happy to pair on this if you want to walk through the options.
P04Nível B1Retrospective contribution
Cenário: Sprint retro. Você precisa contribuir num doc compartilhado pro template "What went well / What didn't / Action items". Foco no time, não em pessoas.
Sua tarefa: 3 itens em cada coluna. Linguagem de retro madura (blameless).
0 palavras

★ Rubrica

  • ✓
    Use Past Simple ou Present Perfect — não Continuous: "We shipped", "We've improved"
  • ✓
    Foco no SISTEMA, não na PESSOA: "The deploy process broke", não "João broke the deploy"
  • ✓
    Action items começam com verbo: "Improve test coverage", não "Better tests needed"
  • ✓
    Cada action tem owner + ETA: "(@gustavo, by next sprint)"

★ Exemplo

**What went well:**
- We shipped 4 features and hit our SLO
- Cross-team coordination with platform improved
- Onboarding the new junior went smoothly

**What didn't:**
- The deploy broke twice — flaky CI exposed in prod
- Standup running long (avg 25 min vs 15 target)
- Stakeholder requests came mid-sprint, derailed planning

**Action items:**
- Stabilize CI pipeline (@platform, by next sprint)
- Move standup to async on Tue/Thu (@gustavo, this week)
- Define mid-sprint scope-change protocol (@PM, next retro)
P05Nível B2Postmortem summary (3 parágrafos)
Cenário: Outage de 1h afetou 15% dos usuários. Você precisa escrever 3 parágrafos pro topo do postmortem público. Tom: factual, blameless, action-oriented.
Sua tarefa:
  • P1: Resumo factual (o que, quando, impacto)
  • P2: Causa raiz (técnica, sem culpar pessoa)
  • P3: O que aprendemos + ações
0 palavras

★ Rubrica

  • ✓
    Voz passiva pra factualidade: "The service was unavailable", não "João broke the service"
  • ✓
    Past Perfect pra timeline: "By the time alerts fired, the queue had backed up for 12 minutes"
  • ✓
    Modal perfects pra arrependimento técnico: "We should have caught this in load testing"
  • ✓
    Action items concretos com owner
  • ✓
    NÃO usa "fault", "blame", "negligence" — vocab de blame culture

★ Exemplo

On 2026-04-15, between 14:00 and 15:08 UTC, the auth service experienced degraded performance, affecting approximately 15% of active users. Users encountered intermittent 503 errors when attempting to log in. The incident lasted 1h08m and was resolved by rolling back to the previous deploy.

The root cause was a connection pool misconfiguration introduced in PR #2341. The new client library defaulted the max connections to 10 — sufficient for staging but exhausted within minutes under production load. Had the change been load-tested against realistic traffic, the issue would have surfaced earlier.

In retrospect, three improvements stand out: first, our load testing harness should mirror production traffic patterns; second, configuration values affecting capacity should be reviewed by SRE before merge; third, our connection pool metrics need tighter alerting thresholds. Action items have been filed in Linear, with owners and timelines.
P06Nível C1RFC summary (executive-friendly)
Cenário: RFC pra migrar pra event-driven architecture. Você escreve a "Executive Summary" de 1-2 parágrafos pra topo do doc. Audiência: VP Eng, leadership team.
Sua tarefa: 1-2 parágrafos densos. Inclua: problema, proposta, tradeoffs, recomendação.
0 palavras

★ Rubrica

  • ✓
    Hedged authority: "This RFC proposes" + "We recommend" ao final
  • ✓
    Discourse markers pra coesão: "That said", "On balance", "Furthermore"
  • ✓
    Quantificação: "reducing latency by ~40%", "with a 6-month migration window"
  • ✓
    Tradeoffs explícitos — não venda só vantagens
  • ✓
    NÃO usa contractions ("we've" → "we have"), NÃO usa phrasal verbs casuais ("look into" → "investigate")

★ Exemplo

**Executive Summary**

This RFC proposes migrating our notification pipeline from a synchronous request-response model to an event-driven architecture using Apache Kafka. Our current system processes ~50K notifications per minute, with p99 latency exceeding our 500ms SLO during peak load. The proposed architecture would decouple producers from consumers, reducing tail latency by an estimated 40% and improving our ability to handle traffic spikes without scaling the entire stack.

That said, the migration carries non-trivial risk and cost. A 6-month implementation window, combined with operational complexity (managing Kafka clusters, schema evolution, consumer offset semantics), means this is a meaningful investment. On balance, we recommend proceeding, with a phased rollout starting with the lowest-criticality notification types in Q3 and full migration by end of Q1 2027. Detailed architecture, alternatives considered, and migration plan follow.
P07Nível C1Performance review self-evaluation
Cenário: Review semestral. Você precisa documentar conquistas + áreas de crescimento + roadmap pessoal. Audiência: seu EM + skip-level (VP).
Sua tarefa: 3 seções (achievements, growth areas, looking ahead). Mostre maturidade — não só sucesso, também self-criticism construtivo.
0 palavras

★ Rubrica

  • ✓
    Verbos de ownership: "led", "drove", "championed", "owned" — não "helped"
  • ✓
    Métricas concretas em cada conquista (ex: "reduced p99 by 35%")
  • ✓
    Self-criticism construtivo: cite áreas + plano. Não "I'm bad at X" mas "X is an area I'm developing through Y"
  • ✓
    Looking ahead: ambição + plano. Demonstra trajetória.
  • ✓
    NÃO comece com "I think I did well" — vago. Use evidência.

★ Exemplo

**H1 2026 Self-Evaluation**

**Highlights this period:**
- Led the migration to Postgres (6 months, $0 customer impact, 35% latency reduction)
- Drove the new RFC process — adopted by 3 other teams, reduced design ambiguity
- Mentored 2 juniors; both received "exceeds" in their reviews

**Areas I'm developing:**
- Async communication on long-running projects — feedback indicated my updates dropped off mid-migration. I've since adopted a weekly written status as standard practice.
- Saying no earlier — I overcommitted to two stretch projects this period. I'm working on scoping conversations earlier with my EM.

**Looking ahead (H2):**
I'd like to grow into a tech leadership role. Concretely, I'd like to scope and lead the new platform team's rollout, working closely with [SRE lead]. I'm open to feedback on whether this aligns with the team's broader priorities.
P08Nível C2Op-ed / engineering blog post
Cenário: Você quer publicar no engineering blog da empresa um post de opinião sobre "by-default async cultures". Audiência: tech community, recruiting reach.
Sua tarefa: 3-4 parágrafos. Use rhetorical questions, parallelism, calls to action. Tom autoritativo mas hedged.
0 palavras

★ Rubrica

  • ✓
    Abre com hook: rhetorical question, contrastive statement, ou narrative
  • ✓
    Estrutura paralela: "We tried X. We tried Y. We tried Z."
  • ✓
    Hedged authority: "What we've learned" em vez de "You should"
  • ✓
    Métricas + narrative: "Six months in, our standup time dropped from 25 to 8 minutes"
  • ✓
    Closing: takeaway concreto + convite à discussão

★ Exemplo

**Why we went async-first — and what we'd do differently**

What if standup is the most expensive meeting we have? That was the question we asked ourselves a year ago, looking at calendars filled with daily 15-minute syncs that, multiplied across 8 engineers, consumed the equivalent of a full headcount per week.

We tried killing standup. We tried shortening it. We tried written-only. What worked, ultimately, was a hybrid: async written updates by 10am, with a 15-minute "office hours" block twice a week for genuinely synchronous problems. Six months in, our average standup time dropped from 25 minutes to 8. More importantly, our engineers reported reclaiming 4-6 hours of focused time per week.

That said, the transition wasn't without cost. New hires struggled to ramp without daily face time. Senior engineers who valued informal syncs felt isolated. We learned, painfully, that "async-first" is not "async-only" — and that the strongest async cultures are deliberate about when sync still matters.

If you're considering a similar shift, three questions worth asking: What problems is your sync-by-default solving that async wouldn't? Where does informal context live? And how will you onboard the next generation? We'd love to hear how others have navigated this — drop your story in the comments.
