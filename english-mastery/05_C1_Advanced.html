01 / Salto estilístico

De fluente a refinado.

Até o B2 você aprendeu o que é certo e o que é errado. No C1, quase tudo é "certo" — mas algumas formas soam mais cultas, mais precisas, mais nativas. Você está saindo da zona da gramática e entrando na zona do estilo.

★ Os 3 sinais de que você atingiu nível C1

Não é só sobre erro — é sobre presença:

  1. Você usa estruturas invertidas pra dramatizar (Never have I seen something like this).
  2. Você suaviza opiniões com hedging em vez de cravar (It might be argued that... / I'd suggest...).
  3. Você varia entre verbo simples ("get") e verbo refinado ("acquire"/"obtain") dependendo do registro.
02 / Inversões

Inversões — quando o auxiliar vem antes.

Em inglês comum, sujeito vem antes do verbo. Em inversões, depois de certas expressões enfáticas no início da frase, a ordem se inverte como se fosse uma pergunta. Soa formal, dramático, literário.

Negativos enfáticos

Never / Rarely / Seldom / Hardly / Scarcely + auxiliar

Normal: I have never seen such beauty.

Invertido: Never have I seen such beauty.

Normal: He had hardly arrived when the meeting started.

Invertido: Hardly had he arrived when the meeting started.

Not only ... but also

"Not only..." enfatizando aumento

Normal: She is not only smart, but also kind.

Invertido: Not only is she smart, but she is also kind.

Normal: He didn't only refuse — he also insulted me.

Invertido: Not only did he refuse — he also insulted me.

No sooner ... than

"Mal eu... e logo..." — sequência rápida

Normal: I had no sooner sat down than the phone rang.

Invertido: No sooner had I sat down than the phone rang.

Only + tempo / circunstância

Only when / Only after / Only by + inversão

Normal: I realized the truth only when she left.

Invertido: Only when she left did I realize the truth.

Normal: You can succeed only by working hard.

Invertido: Only by working hard can you succeed.

Conditional inversion

Subentendendo "if" — eliminando a palavra

Normal: If I had known, I would have helped.

Invertido: Had I known, I would have helped.

Normal: If she were here, this wouldn't happen.

Invertido: Were she here, this wouldn't happen.

Normal: If you should need help, call me.

Invertido: Should you need help, call me.

Drill #1 — Inversões
Múltipla escolha
1. Never ___ such an impressive performance.
2. Hardly ___ down when the bell rang.
3. Not only ___, but she also called the police.
4. ___ the truth, I would have spoken up sooner.
5. Only after she explained twice ___ what she meant.
6. ___ any questions, please contact us.
03 / Cleft sentences

Cleft sentences — destacando com elegância.

"It was John who broke the vase". Em PT seria "FOI O JOÃO que quebrou". Em inglês existe a estrutura formal: It-cleft (com "it") e Wh-cleft (com "what").

It-cleft: It + be + elemento + that/who...

Destacando o sujeito

Normal: John broke the vase.
Cleft: It was John who/that broke the vase.

Destacando o objeto

Normal: I want a coffee.
Cleft: It is a coffee that I want.

Destacando tempo/lugar

Normal: We met in Paris in 2019.
Cleft: It was in 2019 that we met. · It was in Paris that we met.

Wh-cleft: What + cláusula + be + elemento

Foco numa ação ou ideia

Normal: I want to relax.
Cleft: What I want is to relax.

Foco em motivo

Normal: His attitude annoys me.
Cleft: What annoys me is his attitude.

Wh-cleft com "All"

Normal: I just want peace.
Cleft: All I want is peace. (mais coloquial e enfático)

⚠ Quando usar cleft

Use cleft quando você quer destacar enfaticamente uma parte da frase. Em conversa cotidiana é menos comum — mais em escrita formal, debate, contraste.

Useful for: corrections ("It wasn't ME who said that — it was him!"), emphasis, written articles, presentations.

Drill #2 — Cleft sentences
Múltipla escolha
1. ___ called the police, not me.
2. ___ is a vacation, not more work.
3. ___ they got married.
4. ___ his attitude.
5. ___ a quiet evening at home.
04 / Conditionals avançados

Variantes nativas dos conditionals.

No B2 você dominou os 4 padrões básicos. No C1 você aprende as variantes que aparecem em jornal, livro, palestra: provided that, supposing, on condition that, but for, were it not for...

ConectorFunçãoExemplo
provided / providing (that)contanto queI'll go provided that you come too.
on condition thatcom a condição queYou can use my car on condition that you fill up the tank.
supposing / supposee se / vamos suporSupposing she doesn't show up, what then?
unlessa menos que (= if not)We won't go unless it stops raining.
in casecaso / por via das dúvidasTake an umbrella in case it rains.
as long asdesde queAs long as you finish on time, I don't mind.
but for + nounse não fosse / não fossemBut for your help, I would have failed.
were it not for + nounse não fosse (formal)Were it not for him, we'd be lost.
had it not been for + nounse não tivesse sido (passado, formal)Had it not been for the rain, we would have won.
otherwisecaso contrárioHurry up — otherwise we'll be late.

★ Diferença sutil: "in case" não é "se"

Brasileiro confunde "in case" com "if". NÃO são iguais.

"if" — depende
I'll bring an umbrella if it rains. (só se chover)
"in case" — preventivo
I'll bring an umbrella in case it rains. (caso chova — preventivo, levo de qualquer jeito)
Drill #3 — Conditionals avançados
Múltipla escolha
1. I'll lend you the book ___ you return it next week.
2. I'll be there at 7, ___ traffic is bad.
3. Take some cash ___ the card machine doesn't work.
4. ___ your help, I would have failed the project.
5. ___ his support, the project would never have launched.
05 / Subjuntivo

O subjuntivo em inglês — sim, ele existe.

Brasileiros aprendem que "inglês não tem subjuntivo". Mentira. Tem — só é mais sutil. Aparece depois de certos verbos e expressões, sempre na forma base do verbo (sem -s, sem to).

TriggerEstruturaExemplo
It is essential / important / vital that...that + sujeito + base verbIt is essential that he be on time. (não "is")
I suggest / recommend / propose that...that + sujeito + base verbI suggest she arrive early. (não "arrives")
demand / insist / request that...that + sujeito + base verbThey demanded that he resign.
If I were you... (subjuntivo de wish/condicional)were + adj/VIf I were rich... (não "was")
Long live the queen!Frases fixas"Long live", "God save", "Heaven forbid", "Be it so"

★ Inglês americano vs britânico

Subjuntivo é muito mais comum em US English. UK English às vezes usa "should + V" no lugar: I suggest he should arrive early. Ambos corretos.

Drill #4 — Subjuntivo
Múltipla escolha
1. It is essential that everyone ___ present at the meeting.
2. The board demanded that the CEO ___ immediately.
3. If I ___ in your shoes, I'd take the offer.
4. I recommend that she ___ at least 30 minutes early.
5. The judge insisted that the witness ___ interrupted.
06 / Would rather, had better

Would rather e had better — preferência e aviso.

Duas estruturas que parecem similares mas são DIFERENTES. Brasileiro sempre confunde com "would prefer" e "should". Atenção total.

Would rather — preferência

EstruturaSignificadoExemplo
would rather + V (infinitivo)preferiria fazer (mesmo sujeito)I would rather stay home.
would rather + sujeito + past simplepreferiria que outra pessoa fizesse (presente/futuro)I'd rather you didn't smoke here.
would rather + sujeito + past perfectpreferiria que tivesse feito (passado)I'd rather you hadn't said that.
would rather X than Ypreferia X a YI'd rather walk than drive.

Had better — aviso forte

Forma

'd better + V (sem to)

You'd better leave now.

Significado

Conselho com TOM DE AVISO/AMEAÇA. "É melhor fazer (senão...)". Mais forte que "should".

You'd better study, or you'll fail.

Negativa

'd better not + V (NÃO "hadn't better"!)

You'd better not be late.

Drill #5 — Would rather / had better
Múltipla escolha
1. I'd rather ___ home tonight.
2. I'd rather you ___ in the car.
3. I'd rather you ___ him about it yesterday.
4. You'd better ___ now if you want to catch the train.
5. You ___ tell anyone — it's a secret.
07 / Discourse markers

Costurando ideias com discourse markers.

São palavras/frases que organizam o discurso, sinalizam transições, indicam atitude. Sem eles, suas frases ficam soltas. Com eles, você soa coeso e sofisticado.

Adicionar / reforçar

Furthermore · Moreover · What's more · In addition · Besides · On top of that

The plan is risky. Furthermore, it's expensive.

Contrastar

However · Nevertheless · On the other hand · Having said that · That said · Even so

It rained all week. Nevertheless, we had fun.

Concluir / resumir

In conclusion · To sum up · All things considered · On balance · Overall

All things considered, it was a successful event.

Exemplificar

For instance · For example · To illustrate · Such as · Namely · In particular

I love fruits, particularly mangoes.

Causa / efeito

Therefore · As a result · Consequently · Thus · Hence · Owing to

The road was closed. Consequently, we took a detour.

Condicionar / qualificar

Apparently · Allegedly · Supposedly · Presumably · Arguably · Admittedly

Apparently, he didn't get the message.

Drill #6 — Discourse markers
Múltipla escolha
1. The product is reliable. ___, it's affordable.
2. The film was long. ___, I enjoyed every minute.
3. The factory shut down. ___, hundreds lost their jobs.
4. ___ he's planning to quit, but no one's confirmed it yet.
5. The plan looks good. ___, it carries some risks.
08 / Collocations

Collocations — palavras que andam juntas.

Em PT a gente fala "fazer um chá" mas em EN é "make tea" (não "do tea"). Por quê? Porque as palavras combinam de jeitos específicos. Brasileiro tropeça constantemente em collocations — você usa as palavras certas mas a combinação errada.

Make vs Do — a confusão clássica

MAKE — criar / produzir

make a decision · make a mistake · make money · make a phone call · make a plan · make breakfast · make noise · make sense · make a difference · make an effort · make progress

DO — atividade / tarefa

do homework · do the dishes · do exercise · do business · do research · do harm · do a favor · do your best · do the laundry · do nothing · do well

10 / Hedging language

Hedging — falar sem cravar.

Inglês falado/escrito de nível alto raramente afirma com certeza absoluta. Profissionais, acadêmicos, jornalistas suavizam afirmações com hedging language. É a marca registrada do C1+.

★ Por que brasileiro soa rude em inglês profissional

A gente diz "isso está errado" / "você precisa fazer X". Em inglês profissional/diplomático, isso soa AGRESSIVO. Use hedging:

❌ Direto demais (rude)
"This is wrong."
"You need to fix this."
"That's a bad idea."
✅ Com hedging (profissional)
"This might not be quite right."
"You may want to revisit this."
"I'm not sure that would be the best approach."

Hedging por categoria

It seems / It appears that...
parece que
It seems there's been a misunderstanding.
There seems to be...
parece haver
There seems to be an issue.
I'd argue that...
eu argumentaria que
I'd argue that's not entirely accurate.
It could be argued that...
pode-se argumentar que
It could be argued that the data is incomplete.
I'm inclined to think...
tendo a achar que
I'm inclined to think we should wait.
As far as I know...
até onde eu sei
As far as I know, the deadline is Friday.
To my knowledge...
para meu conhecimento
To my knowledge, no decision has been made.
tend to + V
tendem a (suaviza generalização)
People tend to overestimate that.
somewhat / relatively / fairly
um pouco / relativamente
The results are somewhat surprising.
to some extent
até certo ponto
I agree to some extent.
may / might / could
talvez (modal)
This may not be the best approach.
a bit / a little
um pouco (suaviza crítica)
The report is a bit thin on detail.
If I may...
se me permite (super formal)
If I may, I'd like to add a point.
Wouldn't you say...?
você não diria...?
Wouldn't you say it's a bit early?
I'd suggest / I'd recommend
eu sugeriria
I'd suggest we postpone the meeting.
